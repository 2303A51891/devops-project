Core capabilities

Everything you need
to analyse the game

A unified deep learning pipeline that works directly on broadcast video — no sensors, no specialised cameras, no manual annotation.

FPV Shot Extraction
Automatically isolates front pitch view clips from hours of broadcast footage — capturing every ball delivery and batter stroke with precision.
Real-time Ball Tracking
Tracks the cricket ball frame-by-frame from release to bounce, even at speeds up to 90 mph, using YOLOv11 with a shortest-distance algorithm.
Delivery Classification
Classifies every delivery as full-pitched, good-length, or short-pitched by computing the 3D bounce position from a single 2D broadcast camera.
Pitch & Umpire Detection
DETR and YOLO object detectors identify pitch boundaries and umpire position as a secondary validation layer, achieving near-perfect precision.
Replay Identification
Detects the broadcast scorecard to distinguish live deliveries from replays, ensuring datasets contain only unique, non-duplicated ball deliveries.
Coaching & Player Insights
Generates structured datasets of ball deliveries and stroke plays ready for coaching review, commentary generation, and tactical analysis.

How it works

A five-stage pipeline
from broadcast to insight

Each stage builds on the last — from raw video ingestion to classified delivery data ready for coaches and analysts.

01
Video Ingestion
Raw broadcast video loaded directly — no pre-processing, no special encoding requirements.
02
FPV Detection
Dual-stage classifier and object detector run in parallel to isolate front pitch view frames with 100% effective accuracy.
03
Shot Boundary
BackgroundSubtractor identifies scene changes and scorecard detection filters live deliveries from replays.
04
Ball Tracking
YOLOv11 tracks the ball through each FPV shot and detects the 2D bounce point on the pitch surface.
05
Delivery Class.
Zoom estimation converts the 2D bounce position to 3D and classifies each delivery as full, good-length, or short.
